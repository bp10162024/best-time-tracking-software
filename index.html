1. Buddy Punch — Best Overall

Buddy Punch is a comprehensive time tracking software with no shortage of features. Its Starter package includes unlimited time tracking for an unlimited number of projects. The reports are fully featured allowing you to filter and group by user, project, or job code.

If your workforce is remote and in an office, you can limit where they can punch in by IP address or specific in-office device. If your users are instead in the field you can limit them by geofence or specific GPS location. If you need to understand where your employees went during a shift they also offer live GPS tracking.

We can't forget Buddy Punch's webcam feature. It allows you to require that your employees or contractors take a photo when they punch in and out. This allows you to review photos to keep employees honest.

Who should use it:

Buddy Punch is a great option for small to medium sized businesses who are looking for a user-friendly, easy to use time tracking software.

Pros

  • Several ways to punch in: username and password, QR Code scan, 4 digit pin, single sign on (SSO), or face ID with an Apple device.
  • Unlimited number of projects and job tracking with all plans.
  • Limit when, where, and what devices users can punch in and out with.

Cons

  • No free plan
  • No expense tracking
  • Not meant for freelancers

Visit Buddy Punch

2. QuickBooks Time — Best for Mobile Workers

QuickBooks Time is a cloud-based time tracking solution that lets your employees clock in and out from mobile apps and automatically transfer their information to timesheets. You can also set custom rules for PTO and time off requests so that they can manage their vacation, sick time and holidays from the app.

The drag-and-drop tools are designed to help you build schedules and inform your employees of any open shifts or changes. At the end of the pay period, you’ll have a record of your employees’ hours and can sync it to a payroll software for invoicing.

QuickBooks’ geotechnology lets you monitor where your employees are so you can improve dispatching and client billing. It will also remind workers to check in and out when they come to and leave a job site, ensuring all hours are properly tracked.

Who should use it:

QuickBooks Time is the ideal choice if you have a mobile workforce and want to improve productivity and profitability.

Pros

  • Comprehensive geotechnology so you know where your workers are located while on the job
  • Custom PTO management tool to help you manage time off
  • Employee scheduling to improve shift scheduling

Cons

  • No free version
  • Can’t track employee online activity
  • Limited customer support

Visit QuickBooks

3. ClockShark — Best for Construction and Field Service Industries

ClockShark is a time tracking software for small to midsize businesses in the construction and field service industries. It supports three clock-in methods, including mobile apps, kiosks and facial recognition.

Thanks to the built-in GPS, you can see where your employees are while they’re on the clock. Even if they lose internet connection, your workers can still track their time as the software will save the hours they worked and jobs they completed. Once there’s a connection again, the software will sync their data to their account.

There’s also a drag-and-drop schedule editor you can use to create and change work shifts. Any time there is a change in the schedule, ClockShark will automatically inform your team via email or the mobile app. The JobFlow solution offers a list of active and inactive jobs, the clients associated with them, the number of hours worked and the stage of each job.

It’s designed to show employees who are collaborating on the same job what has been completed and what’s in the pipeline. In addition, you can create custom job quotes with photos and documents and generate invoices directly from the quotes and employee timesheets.

Who should use it:

Small businesses in construction and field service that are looking for an alternative to paper timesheets and a robust solution tailored for their industry.

Pros

  • JobFlow solution to track the life cycle of your jobs
  • Drag-and-drop schedule that lets you create and change work shifts
  • Job quote and invoice creator with personalization options

Cons

  • Basic plan is designed for smaller businesses with up to five employees
  • Must upgrade to a higher-level plan for scheduling
  • Higher-level plans are expensive

Visit ClockShark

4. Paymo — Best for Invoicing and Billing

Paymo focuses on time tracking, invoicing and billing. Each task you create in the software will include a start date, end date, description, expected hours to complete, billing type and estimated price. You’ll be able to set alerts when the billable amount of a task is about 90% of the estimate. Once a task is completed, you can create and send an invoice directly from the software.

While the free plan is pretty basic, the higher-level plans let you set live task timers so you can track progress in real time and send reminders and alerts to various team members. There’s also a way to view detailed time reports that show how long tasks are taking and which employees are the most efficient with them.

Paymo’s Kanban boards are ideal if you’d like to streamline workflows and enhance visibility. There are also Gantt charts you can use to plan, schedule and monitor every big or small detail of a project. Plus you can enjoy actionable insights to improve accountability.

Who should use it:

Paymo is worth exploring for businesses that want to be able to track hours, keep tabs on tasks and generate invoices with ease.

Pros

  • Robust Kanban and Gantt features
  • Advanced billing and invoicing features
  • Unlimited invoices on all plans
  • Comment flow on tasks for easy collaboration

Cons

  • Limited users with the basic plan
  • Can be expensive
  • Comes with a steep learning curve

Visit Paymo

5. TimeCamp — Best for Freelancers and Contractors

TimeCamp is a cloud-based time tracking tool that helps all types of workers, including freelancers and contractors, become more productive. While you use it to log the time you spend on various tasks and projects, its tracking tool keeps tabs on the apps and websites you visit. This lets you understand how and where your time is being spent.

You have the option to track your time via desktop, Android or iOS device. Plus you can use a Google Chrome extension. In addition, TimeCamp creates attendance reports that clearly display how long you, and anyone else you work with, worked on a given day. The reports also make it easy to track time by project, client and task time.

Also, TimeCamp integrates with accounting tools, such as QuickBooks and Xero so you can track your clients accurately and budget for them accordingly.

Who should use it:

TimeCamp is a solid pick if you’re a freelancer or contractor. It can help you identify and resolve any habits that take away from your productivity and accurately measure your profitability.

Pros

  • Workers can track time from mobile, desktop and web apps
  • Allows for integrations with popular business tools, including Trello, Asana, QuickBooks and Xero
  • Reporting tools to help monitor attendance

Cons

  • No invoicing on Free and Basic plan
  • Timesheet approvals only available on Pro and Enterprise plans
  • No phone support
  • No integrations on Free plan

Visit TimeCamp

6. Deputy — Best for Staying Compliant With Labor Laws

Deputy has no shortage of useful features to help you stay compliant, save costs and improve efficiency. You can create and assign ongoing or one-off tasks or checklists to individual employees. Once you do, you’ll be able to monitor them through a central dashboard with a color code that displays which tasks were completed and which ones are still pending.

The software’s time clock app gives managers insight on where employees are so they can ensure the ideal coverage and easily find replacements as needed. There’s also a shift scheduling tool to help you comply with federal, state and local labor laws.

You can schedule required breaks, paid time off and overtime. Since the app flags missed or unscheduled breaks, you’ll be able to easily reduce compliance risks. Deputy also offers a timesheet feature that can calculate employee wages based on employment contracts or local labor laws.

Who should use it:

If you’d like to ensure your small business stays compliant with labor laws, Deputy can come in handy.

Pros

  • Employees can clock in and out digitally
  • Shift scheduling tool to simplify labor law compliance
  • Automatic wage calculations according to local labor laws or employment contracts

Cons

  • Very basic free plan
  • Must invest in Enterprise plan to enjoy the forecasting feature
  • User interface could be improved

Visit Deputy

7. Toggl Track — Best Free Plan

Formerly Toggl, Toggl Track is a time tracking app with a generous free plan. If you’re a freelancer, solopreneur or very small business, the free plan is sure to exceed your expectations. It’s full of basic time tracking features, including a timeline, auto-tracker, idle detection, off-line time tracking and weekly reports.

Best of all, there are no limits to how many projects you can create or how many clients you can track. Of course, you can enjoy more advanced features if you upgrade to the higher-level plans. Several of these include calendar integration, project templates, time tracking audits and priority support.

Since Toggl Track is essentially a timer, it’s a breeze to use. All you have to do is turn it on and let it count seconds and minutes. Once you end a timing session, the total amount of time will be saved to your account.

Who should use it:

For a free software that offers impressive insights and reporting, Toggl Track should be on your radar.

Pros

Cons

9. Time Doctor — Best for Monitoring Employee Productivity

Time Doctor is a cloud-based time tracking software with an emphasis on productivity monitoring. Its tools let you analyze how employees manage their time and alert you when they’re wasting it.

You can set alerts and reminders to increase productivity by stating a time limit when workers can be idle. Once they exceed this limit, they’ll receive a pop-up that asks them if they’re still working. In the event they don’t respond after 60 seconds, the timer will stop recording.

There’s also a tool that captures employees’ screens at random times so you can see what they’re working on. In addition, you can track their website activities to find out if they visit any sites that hinder their productivity while they’re at work. The daily and weekly productivity reports can be useful as well.

Who should use it:

Time Doctor makes sense if you have a remote or hybrid workforce or are simply looking for a way to increase your productivity.

What Is Time Tracking Software?

Time tracking software helps employers and workers keep track of time spent on various tasks, projects and other deliverables. You can use it to stay organized, regardless of your industry or job responsibilities. With a quality time tracking solution, you’ll find it easy to pinpoint distractions and make smart business decisions that allow you to become more productive and profitable.

Benefits of Time Tracking Software

Time tracking software offers a number of noteworthy advantages, such as:

  • Increased productivity: If your team struggles to meet deadlines or you’re worried they’re slacking off, time tracking software can help you pinpoint inefficiencies and create a more productive workforce.
  • More accurate quoting: Because you’ll be able to record how long each task or job takes, time tracking software may take the time and guesswork out of accurate quoting.
  • Easier prioritization: With time tracking software, you’ll have a better idea of how to allocate your resources to tackle the highest-priority projects first.
  • Improved rewards and retention: Time tracking solutions will let you home in on your greatest employees so you can reward and retain them.

How To Choose Time Tracking Software

Not all time tracking software is created equal. To help you find the ideal option for your unique situation, consider the following.

Essential Features

  • Time tracking: This lets you keep track of how your employees spend their time while on the job.
  • Timesheets: Timesheets calculate the work your employees perform in terms of billable hours so you can simplify billing and invoicing.
  • Reporting: Most time tracking software creates reports on active employee hours, tasks employees complete and more.
  • Integrations: Integrations with project management and communication tools such as Salesforce, Asana and Slack are important if you’d like to maximize performance.
  • Mobile app: With a mobile app, you and your employees can manage your time from anywhere.

Consider Your Business Processes

The way you run your business and perform your work will determine the best time tracking solution. It’s a good idea to ask yourself these questions:

  • Do you work in an office or away from the office?
  • Are you a freelancer or solopreneur or a small to medium-sized business with employees?
  • Do you use Trello, Asana or other project management apps?
  • How do you communicate with your employees and clients?

If you’re typically away from the office and out in the field, for example, a robust mobile app should be your priority. In the event you’re a small business with several employees, investing in a paid time tracking solution may make more sense than if you’re self-employed with no employees.

Stand-alone Software vs. Suites

A stand-alone time tracker is a basic tool that lets you record the time spent on projects and tasks. In most cases, you’ll need to manually enter your time and categorize it. A time tracking suite, on the other hand, is more robust and offers bells and whistles, such as schedule management, geo-tracking, easy integrations and more.

What Does Time Tracking Software Cost?

When it comes to pricing for time tracking software, it varies greatly. Typically, however, most solutions will charge you a flat monthly rate plus a rate per user. Factors including specific features and the number of employees you have will dictate what you pay for a time tracking tool. Keep in mind that sometimes, you won’t be able to find pricing information online and will need to reach out to the company directly for a custom quote.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is time tracking software?

Time tracking software allows employees to record the time they spend on various tasks or projects. Most solutions include extra features, such as invoicing, reporting and productivity monitoring.

What does time tracking software do?

Time tracking software keeps track of work hours. It provides insight on how much time was spent on various tasks, projects and clients. Most solutions also offer extras, such as scheduling tools and invoicing.

Do I need time tracking software?

Whether you need time tracking software depends on your unique business and goals. If you’re a small business who would like to improve employee productivity, it may make sense. On the flip side, if you’re a freelancer who is starting out, you may be able to keep track of your time manually without a solution.

What are the cons of time tracking software?

Typically, time tracking software with robust features will cost you a flat monthly rate plus a fee per user. Also, some time tracking software comes with a steep learning curve, so it might take some time and effort to get used to it and unlock its full potential.

Are there other ways to track employee time?

While it’s convenient, you don’t need a time tracking software to track employee time. Instead, you may use a spreadsheet, such as Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets. Another option is to install time clocks where employees can clock in and out. In addition, you can try geofencing or GPS tracking apps to keep track of employee time while they’re away from the office.
